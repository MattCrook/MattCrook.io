About Me

My personality has always been ambitious, driven, and goal oriented. As a former athlete, playing both semi-pro baseball and golf, former professional musician, former personal trainer and strength coach, former music industry professional, and now software engineer, I know the meaning of discipline, but I also know I love learning! Previous to software engineering, my career in the music business started as a project manager, and ended up leading me to become a data analyst in Income Tracking Analytics. In that role, I worked regularly with large data in Hadoop using software like Tableau, and open-source SQL engines like Apache Hive and Impala, and later Google Cloud. I was also exposed to utilizing the underlying platform services and infrastructure using Kubernetes, wrote scripts to fetch data from various API's, and worked with developers to build tools for process automation and business forecasting.

It was during this role that I started to teach myself programming, and immediately fell in love with it. So much so, that I went all in and decided to attend a bootcamp called Nashville Software School. My focus there included Javascript, Node.js, and Python. This was a fairly substantial career shift, but one I am happy I made as I get to solve problems everyday, learn everyday, and keep my search for a challenge always satisfied!

Outside of work, I enjoy being active, going to the gym, being involved in the community as well as going to conferences, and anything that involves learning something new or challenging myself. I also really enjoy mentoring new students and helping people bring out the best in themselves.

Volunteer Work

PyTennessee 2020

PyTn is hosted at Nashville School of Law and is is a yearly regional Python conference that is a developer-focused Python conference with talk topics running the gamut between beginner and advanced, and from web development to data science and machine learning. I was responsible for possibly the most important part of the conference--giving the speakers the help they needed and keeping the conference moving smoothly. I participated in helping in set-up, tear down, checking guests in, and moving items as necessary.

Music City Code 2019

The Music City Tech conference is held at Nossi College of Art in Nashville, TN and is a conference that caters to developers and touches on all aspects of software and website development. The Conference is focused on quality over quantity, with three sub-conferences: Music City Code, Music City Agile, and Music City Data. As a volunteer, I was responsible for set-up before the conference and checking guests in, as well as catering to the speakers, making sure they had what they needed for their talks, getting them set up for their talks, as well as any duty necessary to make sure the conference ran as smoothly as possible.
