About

My personality has always been ambitious, driven, and goal oriented. As a former athlete, playing both semi-pro baseball and golf, former professional musician, former personal trainer and strength coach, former music industry professional, and now software engineer, I know the meaning of discipline, but I also know I love learning! Previous to software engineering, my career in the music business started as a project manager, and ended up leading me to become a data analyst in Income Tracking Analytics. In that role, I worked regularly with large data in Hadoop then later utilizing cloud providers and services such as Google Cloud Platform, using visualization software like Tableau, and open-source SQL engines like Apache Hive and Impala, and writing scripts in python. I was also exposed to utilizing the underlying platform services and infrastructure using Kubernetes and Ansible, also wrote scripts to fetch data from various API's, and worked with developers to build tools for process automation and business forecasting.

It was during this role that I started to teach myself programming, and immediately fell in love with it. So much so, that I went all in and decided to attend a bootcamp called Nashville Software School. My focus there included Javascript, React, Node.js, Python, and the Django framework. This was a fairly substantial career shift, but one I am happy I made as I get to solve problems everyday, learn everyday, and keep my search for a challenge always satisfied!

Currently, I am working as a Site Reliability Engineer where my day to day duties can range from optimizing existing infrastructure to support removing technical debt and preventing future incidents, tracking core metrics and solving bottlenecks proactively, building/ improving tools and features that refine or enhance sustainability, monitoring, and logging; as well as resolving incidents within internal SLAs, and am the lead for everything surrounding testing, test coverage, testing standards and testing best practices. I regularly use technologies like Terraform, Kubernetes, Helm, Jenkins, Docker, python, and Bash scripting, as well as tools like Sentry.io, New Relic, Snyk, Styra, Coveralls, SauceLabs, Divvy Cloud, and Codefresh.

What lead me to becoming an SRE was my previous knowledge and interest of the entire development cycle and workflow, as well as passion and curiosity for infrastructure. Previous to my current role, I was a full-stack software engineer, where my day to day would range from front end work like creating an entirely new feature or page, or refining an existing one, manipulating and partitioning data, data visualizations, and much more. In addition, I worked on back end functionality like creating an ERD and wire framing to plan for a new feature/ migration/ API endpoint, implementing the specified database structure and API contract in models and views, adding or editing existing functionality in a controller (view if this is Django), building endpoints for a Flask API, refining back end validations, testing for site reliability and security, and everything in between like maintaining the CI/CD pipeline in deployment to a development environment and production, health checking our Kubernetes clusters, changing/ creating IAM permissions as needed, and maintaining and managing expectations of stakeholders through clear communication regarding sprint status, bandwidth, and feasibility of any particular goal.

In addition, my previous professional experience in data visualizations using python and Tableau, utilization of Hadoop and later GCP, knowledge of income tracking reporting, recovery, and analytics, usage of SQL for queries using various drivers and Power BI, means I am well versed in many technologies, as well as a very quick learner.

Outside of work, I enjoy being active, going to the gym, being involved in the community as well as going to conferences, and anything that involves learning something new or challenging myself. I also really enjoy mentoring new students and helping people bring out the best in themselves.

Volunteer Work

I was responsible for possibly the most important part of the conference--giving the speakers the help they needed and keeping the conference moving smoothly. Music City Code 2019

The Music City Tech conference is held at Nossi College of Art in Nashville, TN and is a conference that caters to developers and touches on all aspects of software and website development. The Conference is focused on quality over quantity, with three sub-conferences: Music City Code, Music City Agile, and Music City Data. As a volunteer, I was responsible for set-up before the conference and checking guests in, as well as catering to the speakers, making sure they had what they needed for their talks, getting them set up for their talks, as well as any duty necessary to make sure the conference ran as smoothly as possible.
